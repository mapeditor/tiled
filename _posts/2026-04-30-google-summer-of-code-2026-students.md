Google has announced the accepted projects for [Google Summer of Code
2026][gsoc2026], and I'm happy to say that Tiled will be mentoring two
contributors this summer:

- **Kanishka** ([kanishka0411][]) on [Enhanced World
  Editing][world-editing-wiki], making working with multi-map worlds a lot more
  convenient and direct.

- **Dagon Williams** ([UltraDagon][]) on the [New Hardware
  Renderer][renderer-wiki], bringing Qt Quick based GPU rendering into the main
  editor. Dagon will be mentored by Adam Turk (biggeruniverse), who started the
  original Java version of Tiled and co-authored it with me.

### About the Selection

We received a lot of applications this year (29 proposals submitted!), and with
quite a few good candidates. Since we could only accept one contributor per
project and we had a very limited set of mentors, several great and clearly
motivated candidates did not make it in.

So to everyone who applied: thank you for the time you put in, and please don't
take a "no" as a verdict on your work. Several of you have already landed pull
requests in Tiled, and I hope you'll stick around. Your contributions are very
much appreciated and you can learn regardless of GSoC!

### What's Next

The [Community Bonding Period][timeline] runs from **May 1 through May 24**, with coding officially
starting **May 25**. During bonding we'll:

- Have a kickoff call together, either this weekend or Monday, whenever everyone can make it.
- Go through your proposals in detail, refine them where useful.
- Generally get to know each other a little better!

We're looking forward to a productive summer with you!
