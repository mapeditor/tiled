Tiled is participating in the [Google Summer of Code][] (GSoC) for the second time! We had a very
successful first time back in 2017, where [3 student projects][gsoc2017] were instrumental in
shaping the eventual [Tiled 1.1 release][].

Since the [announcement of the Mentoring Organizations][gsoc2026orgs], we've experienced a wave of
interested students, many starting to work on open issues to get familiar with the codebase. Some
have already submitted multiple pull requests. It will take some days for me to get around to all of
them, but I really appreciate the interest and try to respond as good as I can.

### Project Proposals

Applications for GSoC contributors will be open March 16-31. We've provided a list of [project
ideas][] but are also open to any other project. Note that we're not a large organization and can
unfortunately only mentor up to 3 contributors as part of GSoC 2026!

Until then, please use the time to get familiar with Tiled development, and to let us get to know
you as well. Your help will be very appreciated and you can learn a lot from the experience, even if
you don't end up doing a project with us this summer.

### Call for Mentors

At the moment we have two mentors. I am mostly available full time and
[dogboydog](https://github.com/dogboydog), a regular contributor to Tiled since a while, has also
agreed to be a mentor. We'd like to be able to accept more proposals, so if you're reading this and
have some experience with Tiled and the related technologies (C++, Qt), please consider if this
might be something for you and get in touch!

### Thanks!

Thanks to Google for running this program and putting their trust in our organization for the second
time! Also thanks to dogboydog for helping as a mentor! Finally thanks to all interested
contributors who are already making an impact on future Tiled releases!

Talking about releases, today I am putting Tiled 1.12 on "string freeze" so translators can make
sure the translations are complete. It's been a while since the last new features release and [the
changelog][] is huge. It would be good to get this out before GSoC starts!
